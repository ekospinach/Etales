General Introduction

eTales is a business simulation reflecting vividly the horizontal and vertical competition of Manufacturers and Retailers in the consumer goods industry.

It mirrors the power dynamics in a competitive and changing market influenced by the modern communication channels, i.e. consumer empowerment through social media and its impact on power dynamics (brand communities), which results in channel migration and impacts Manufacturers and Retailers profitability.

Real players are seeking competitive advantage over their rivals in the industry. Administrator decides and announces what criteria are used to determine the winner. So your aim would be to develop and implement strategy and tactics allowing your team to get best scores and outplay your competition.

Overview

eTales recreates a fast growing emerging market in a wiki environment. There are two markets: urban and rural, and two categories: health/beauty and elecssories.

25 to 30 people are separated into 5 team, 3 represent manufacturers and 2 brick-and-mortar retailers, modern retailer chains. Manufacturers can sell through brick-and-mortar retailers and online channels.

There are 3 virtual teams in eTales managed by computer: Manufacturer4, Retailer 3 (representing the traditional trade) and eMall to make the market more dynamic.

Every decision represents 6 months in the real world. Negotiations are set up between manufacturers and retailers via face to face communication and online discussion.

At the beginning, every team is granted the same amount of operational budget.

Urban market is ahead of rural market in terms of development. Urban is more stable, rural is more dynamic, prone to catch up urban.

Both markets are dependent, that is:

  • Brand awareness/preferences in Rural are affected by brand performancein Urban;
  • Store perception in Rural is affected by store performance in Urban.

As a result, consumers/shoppers in Rural mimic behaviour in Urban.

Game structure

Game Structure - Goods Flow or Supply Chain

3 Manufacturer teams and 2 Retailer teams compete in two different markets: Urban and Rural.

Virtual supplier (Manufacturer 4) is generated by computer and serves primarily two purposes:

  • it supplies Retailer 1 and Retailer 2 with their private labels
  • and if real suppliers fail to satisfy consumer demand with innovation, it may launch new innovated products distributed through eMall

There are two absolutely different virtual retail players:

  • Retailer 3 which is a traditional retail, in parallel to retailer 1 and retailer 2
  • eMall which is a new emerging on-line distribution channel

Consumers as Shoppers

Manufacturer Decision

As a Manufacturer in eTales, you need make the following business decisions:

  1. Products portfolio
  2. Production planning and pricing
  3. Allocate advertising budget in different channel at SKU level
  4. Category Decisions
  5. Technology

Assortment decisions should be completed well in advance and prior to the rest of Manufacturer decisions, so that Retailers have enough time to complete their decision and place orders timely. Administrator will notify you when this step should be completed.

You may return to change data in previous steps anytime until you commit the whole decision.

Retailer Decision

If you are in a Retailer team, you need to make following decisions:

  1. Advertising
  2. Market
  3. Private Labels
  4. Order, Pricing and Shelf Allocation of each SKU

Negotiations

During the course of the simulation you will have to negotiate with your business partners. Your session Facilitator will explain you rules, order and time of your negotiations. Whatever the specifics of your session, you will need to record your deals in the system.

Reports

You will be given the following reports:

  • Financial Statement
  • Cash Flow
  • Manufacturer Profitability by Channel
  • Retailer Profitability by Supplier

The upfront reports are shown at national level with totals for all markets. You may drill down reports at different level at Category, Brand or SKU. Select Market from the selection list a Period if you wish to look at history data.

Market Studies

To provide you with basic market information, you will be provided the following market studies:

  • Brand Awareness
  • Brand Market Share (Volume)
  • Brand Market Share (Value)
  • Average Net Market Price
  • Brand Visibility Share
  • Consumer Off-Take (sales)

You may sort table by any data column, just click column's header. Clicking the header again will reverse sort order. You may wish to use Category and/or Market filters on top to narrow \ widen your view on data.
